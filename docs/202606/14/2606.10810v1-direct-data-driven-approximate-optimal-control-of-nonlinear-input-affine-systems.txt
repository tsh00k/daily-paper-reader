Abstract: In this paper, we combine a data-driven system representation with a framework to systematically construct (approximate) solutions to nonlinear optimal control problems. By immersing the unknown dynamics into an extended state space, solutions are characterised via purely data-dependent algebraic conditions. This allows us to design dynamic state-feedback controllers with local stability and performance guarantees for unknown nonlinear, input-affine systems directly using data, without explicitly identifying the dynamics. 

Keywords: Data-based control, optimal control, stability of nonlinear systems 

1. INTRODUCTION The problem of designing controllers directly from mea-sured data has been receiving increasing attention in re-cent years. A framework to design state-feedback con-trollers for linear time-invariant (LTI) systems, which utilises Willems’ Fundamental Lemma (see Willems et al. (2005)) to represent the closed-loop system directly using data has been introduced in De Persis and Tesi (2020). Various approaches have been presented to extend the ideas to classes of nonlinear systems. For more details and the relation to nonlinear system identification, see De Persis and Tesi (2023); Monshizadeh et al. (2025) and references therein. The main focus has so far been on data-driven stabilisation of nonlinear systems. Optimal control has been considered for bilinear systems (Yuan and Cort´ es (2022)) and for discrete input-affine systems via online linearisation (Dai et al. (2025)). Data-based (approximate) solutions to optimal control problems using different ap-proaches to parametrise unknown nonlinear dynamics have been proposed in Yu et al. (2022), Prag et al. (2022) and references therein. Even in the model-based case, solving infinite-horizon non-linear optimal control problems is generally challenging. The solution is characterised via the Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellmann (HJB) equation (Liberzon (2011)). With the exception of certain special cases, closed-form solutions of this nonlinear partial differential equation (PDE) are not available and numerical solutions are computation-ally expensive to obtain. For optimal control problems involving nonlinear, input-affine systems, Sassano et al. (2018) presents a framework to construct (approximate) solutions without involving the explicit solution of any PDE. Namely, via an immersion of the nonlinear dynamics

into an extended state-space, approximate solutions are constructed systematically, based solely on algebraic ma-trix conditions, with any matrix satisfying these conditions referred to as an algebraic P solution. Moreover, the level of approximation is quantifiable and, thus, also minimis-able. More details about the framework and its relation to alternative methods for nonlinear optimal control can be found in Sassano et al. (2021) and references therein. In this work, we combine the discussed line of direct data-driven control with the aforementioned “framework of algebraic P solutions”. This allows us to determine (approximate) solutions to nonlinear optimal control prob-lems involving unknown input-affine systems directly using measured data. The algebraic P solutions framework has been extended to other control problems involving PDEs, including classes of stochastic control problems (Gong et al. (2025), L2-disturbance attenuation (Sassano and Astolfi (2012)), and differential games (Mylvaganam et al. (2015)). Hence, the presented approach lays the founda-tions for the direct data-driven solution of a wide class of nonlinear control problems. The paper is organised as follows. The considered problems are defined in Section 2. A data-driven system representa-tion is introduced in Section 3. Section 4 proposes control design methods via purely data-dependent algebraic con-ditions. The results are demonstrated via an example in Section 5. Concluding remarks are provided in Section 6.

Remark 6. While we consider the noise-free case, i.e. 

system (1) and data (13) are not affected by noise, the underlying approach extends to other control problems in-volving the solution of PDEs, including L2-disturbance attenuation (Sassano and Astolfi (2012)). An important question in data-driven control is how to represent un-known systems based on noisy data. Considering process noise, several system representation approaches have been proposed, see e.g. De Persis and Tesi (2023). We expect that these can be applied to derive alternative data-driven algebraic conditions for the problems considered herein. Considering measurement noise, if the type of system dy-namics is known, the approach to treat measurement noise similarly to process noise introduced in De Persis and Tesi (2020) extends without difficulties to nonlinear systems (De Persis and Tesi (2023)). Like many related results ( e.g. Guo et al. (2022); Madeira and Correia (2024)), our results depend on measurements of ˙x, which are often un-available in practice and might have to be approximated via filters. Hence, the samples ˙xd are most likely to be affected by measurement noise. While a system representation as in Guo et al. (2022) can be used instead, note that the presented methods have an inherent degree of robustness as demonstrated in the example in Section 5. This is due to the construction based on the Lyapunov function (23) .

CONCLUSION We consider the problem of designing approximately op-timal controllers for optimal control problems involving nonlinear, input-affine systems with unknown dynamics directly using data. First, we introduce a direct data-driven system representation that is amenable to our prob-lem formulation. This is then utilised in the context of a framework to systematically construct locally stabilising controllers and approximate solutions to optimal control problems via system immersion and algebraic conditions. The presented methods do not require the explicit solution of any nonlinear PDE, but solutions are instead charac-terised via purely data-dependent algebraic conditions.
